Moab trails (Jeep Badge of Honor, RR4W ≤ 6)

Reference for the 2026 trip. RR4W numbers are from the Red Rock 4-Wheelers EJS 2025 Trail Grid PDF (same scale as Participants.md). Trails in the Jeep Badge of Honor app are marked with the green BOH badge () in headings and the table—that is a trip-pack placeholder, not the official Jeep trademark artwork. Confirm check-in rules in the Jeep app. Trail photos are omitted; use linked guides and your own scouting shots. Typical times on each card are rough ranges for a private group (air-down, lunch, photos, optional obstacles)—slower than a tight club run, faster than a photo safari.

Rig shorthand (for “hardest parts” below):
  • 4Runner — stock-ish suspension, 33″ tires (example: Sean’s questionnaire).
  • JL — stock Jeep Wrangler 4-door: shorter tires than ideal for RR4W “recommended” sizes on some trails, moderate wheelbase.
  • Gladiator — lifted on 35s, typically best clearance of the three; still watch breakover on long-wheelbase climbs.

Glenn’s Moab shortlist (personal picks)

Aligned with this trip’s Moab block May 7–9, 2026 (daily itinerary — Thu / Fri / Sat as the three Moab trail days). The idea is an RR4W ramp: start on a 4, step up to a 5, then a big 6 day—while still on Hell’s slickrock, experienced drivers can peel off onto harder optional lines when they want them.

Baby Lion’s Back (slickrock warm-up): same Sand Flats fee area as Hell’s / Fins—run Wed May 6 evening after Moab arrival if time and daylight allow, or Thu May 7 morning before Tip-toe through Hell so the group gets a short taste of mesa slickrock before the main Hell’s day (not stacked as a late add-on after Tip-toe).

Suggested trails by Moab day:
  1. Moab day 1 — Thu May 7: Hell’s Revenge - Tip-toe through Hell RR4W 4 (RR4W “Tip-Toe Trip”; same Hell’s slickrock, bypasses emphasized) — easier baseline for the group; more experienced rigs can still run harder named obstacles (Hell’s Gate, Escalator, etc.) when everyone’s comfortable—treat those as full “6” moves for spotting and equipment.
  2. Moab day 2 — Fri May 8: Wipe-Out Hill RR4W 5
  3. Moab day 3 — Sat May 9: Top of the World RR4W 6 — long highway + rim mileage; leave slack in the day.

Jeep Badge of Honor check-in is usually tied to Hell’s Revenge as a trail name in the app—confirm current rules; you’re still on the Hell’s mesa even when running the Tip-Toe line.

Interesting alternates (swap in or save for a future Moab pass if the group reorders): Hell’s Revenge RR4W 6 (commit to the full rated-6 day from the start), Sevenmile Rim RR4W 4, 3-D RR4W 4, and the rest of the quick comparison table below.

Two trails in one day? The three-day layout above assumes one primary trail per Moab calendar day. Sevenmile Rim and Wipe-Out Hill share some country on certain GPS variants—running both full loops the same day is usually too much. Baby Lion’s Back + Tip-toe through Hell on May 7 is intentional if Baby Lion is a morning warm-up (short hit, then the full Tip-toe day). Tip-toe + Fins and Things same day is a fast-group move.

Lost World RR4W 3

New to EJS in 2024. Runs between Rainbow Terrace and Lost World Butte, southeast of Tenmile Canyon. Surfaces mix county road, dirt, sand, and slickrock; named waypoints include Mini-Hell’s Gate, Gun Site Rock, Serpent Arch, and Juniper Arch. RR4W calls out steep climbs/descents and “tippy” sections despite the low number rating—terrain feel can exceed a “3” in places if you are new to off-camber slickrock.

Typical time: about 4–7 hours wheel-to-wheel for a mixed group (distance varies by exact loop; add time for approach roads and staging).

Often combined with: usually its own half- or full-day. If you want two tastes of the Sand Flats world, some groups add a short out-and-back the same calendar day—see Porcupine Rim (can make a long day together).

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Usually fine; watch breakover on short ledge transitions and any off-camber slickrock—stock sidesteps and long rear overhang can drag.
  • Stock JL: Good breakover; door sheetmetal in any narrow brush/rock pinch; tire height is the main limiter on steeper ledges.
  • Gladiator 35s: Least concern; still mind tippy feel for passengers—pick lines slowly.

Fins and Things RR4W 4

Classic Sand Flats slickrock loop east of Hell’s Revenge: rolling Navajo fins, sandy approaches, and roller-coaster climbs including “Frenchies Fin” and “Kenny’s Climb.” About 25 mi total / ~13 mi off highway per RR4W. Great first full trail day for a mixed group.

Typical time: about 3–5 hours on the fins (add Sand Flats entry and air-down).

Often combined with: same-day Hell’s Revenge only if the group wants a very long Sand Flats day (~8–12+ hours total); more commonly a dedicated Fins day then Hell’s another day. Baby Lion’s Back is a common evening add-on or next-day morning warm-up in the same fee area (see Glenn’s shortlist for May 6 / May 7 timing).

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Steep climbs with long overhangs—scrape risk on transitions; pick bypass lines where offered.
  • Stock JL: Generally well matched; short wheelbase helps on tight fin climbs.
  • Gladiator: Watch tail swing on switchback-style slickrock turns.

Wipe-Out Hill RR4W 5

Scenic ~43 mi / ~19 mi off highway loop south of Canyonlands Airport and west of Hwy 191 through Bartlett Wash, Tusher Canyon, Courthouse Pasture, and Big Mesa. RR4W highlights: sandy hill early, wet/narrow Tusher Wash rock course (wide/long rigs fight sheetmetal), Wicked Ledge, short steep Wipe-Out Hill, and Rattlesnake Hill bumps. High “variety” trail—geology and views are a big payoff.

Typical time: about 5–8 hours for the full RR4W-style loop with a relaxed group.

Often combined with: Sevenmile Rim when leaders include the Tusher / Big Mesa fork—you overlap the same country (still a full day). Otherwise usually standalone west of town.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Tusher Wash pinstriping and step damage risk; rear locker helps on sandy pulls per RR4W.
  • Stock JL: Narrow wash is your friend vs width; still watch rock sliders on Wicked Ledge / ledgy climbs.
  • Gladiator 35s: Width + wheelbase in Tusher—spot carefully; lockers help on Rattlesnake Hill.

Hell’s Revenge RR4W 6

Premier Sand Flats slickrock route (~16 mi total, ~12 off highway): steep climbs/descents, famous optional obstacles (Hell’s Gate, Escalator, Mickey’s Hot Tub, etc.) with bypasses on many hard spots. Intersects the Slickrock Bike Trail in places. RR4W lists 35+ tires and front + rear lockers—plan spotting and conservative lines for stocker 33s.

Typical time: about 4–7 hours depending on how many optional obstacles you queue for and how busy Sand Flats is.

Often combined with: Fins and Things on a monster Sand Flats day (only for fast groups), or—more realistically—Baby Lion’s Back as a May 6 evening or May 7 pre–Tip-toe warm-up (see Glenn’s shortlist). Hell’s Revenge - Tip-toe through Hell is the usual lower-number alternative to full Hell’s, not usually stacked back-to-back with it.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Escalator / steep V-notches, brake traction on downgrades, and breakover at the base of ledges; use bypasses freely.
  • Stock JL: Traction is often fine on Navajo slickrock; exposure and tall center of gravity matter more than raw grip—avoid hot-tub stunts without experience.
  • Gladiator 35s: Still long—breakover on double-apex climbs; lockers make the day relaxed on rated 6 moves.

Poison Spider Mesa RR4W 6

Climbs from Potash Road (SR 279) onto the mesa northwest of town (~37 mi / ~16 off highway). Highlights include switchbacks, sandy pulls, the Waterfall ledge series, optional V-notch photo line, and big rim views (Jeep Arch, Moab below). Do not confuse with the “Where Eagles Dare” add-on (RR4W 7) unless the whole group wants that tier.

Typical time: about 5–8 hours for the mesa loop at a social pace.

Often combined with: the scenic Potash Road drive to/from the trailhead (same SR 279 corridor—not a second “trail,” but part of the same outing). Treat as its own day versus Hell’s Revenge / Fins (different side of town).

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Waterfall ledges and sand before them—momentum + protection for oil pan / sliders.
  • Stock JL: Good for tight ledge steps; 35s+ recommended by RR4W for a reason—expect careful wheel placement.
  • Gladiator 35s: Length on switchbacks and V-notch optional squeeze—spot the rear bumper path.

Top of the World RR4W 6

Long day: RR4W lists ~64 mi pavement + ~25 off pavement to a dramatic overlook on Waring Mesa above Fisher Valley / Onion Creek (Dewey Bridge area on Utah 128). Much is scenic approach; the 4×4 section mixes broken rock, loose climbs, and shelf steps to the rim. Often windy. Confirm early-spring snow/mud on La Sal–side approaches before committing.

Typical time: about 8–11 hours door-to-door from Moab (long highway connectors + rim section + lunch).

Often combined with: usually standalone because of mileage. If you are already on the river corridor, plan Dome Plateau as a different day (both are big).

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Shelf steps + loose rock—long wheelbase can teeter; need solid skid package and careful line choice.
  • Stock JL: Traction can work with lockers; fuel range and long day fatigue matter as much as obstacles.
  • Gladiator 35s: Best matched to RR4W equipment call—still watch descending shelf traction when rocks are wet.

Steel Bender RR4W 6

Mill Creek country between Moab and the La Sals (~30 mi / ~15 off highway): multiple creek crossings, then sustained shelved hill climbs, optional “The Wall” loop with a slickrock fin, and a historically tricky rocky descent RR4W warns “continues to defy” maintenance—then more shelves and a slickrock descent to South Mill Creek. Rating has bounced with erosion—treat as a solid 6 mentally even when the grid says 6.

Typical time: about 5–8 hours with creek play and careful shelf spotting.

Often combined with: usually standalone east of town (different vibe than Sand Flats). Some locals pair a short Mill Creek scenic drive with dinner in Moab—not the same as the full trail.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: RR4W lists 35s + both lockers—expect shelf hill traction struggles and possible winch points if wet; skip “Wall” loop if unsure.
  • Stock JL: Creek crossings OK; shelved climbs are the filter—spot every hump, protect diffs.
  • Gladiator 35s + lift: Best fit on paper; still respect the maintained-but-nasty descent—long wheelbase can swap ends if you rush.

Porcupine Rim RR4W 6

Starts from Sand Flats Road (~9.5 mi from Moab), drops to terraces above Negro Bill Canyon, then climbs to rim overlooks including Picture Rock over Castle Valley. RR4W lists ~32 mi total / ~6 mi off highway; they warn the route is continuously rough (bedrock knobs and ledges) despite big views. Spring snow or mud is possible at Easter elevation.

Typical time: about 3–5 hours out-and-back at a photo-friendly pace.

Often combined with: other Sand Flats hits the same trip week—e.g. Lost World or Fins and Things on a different day (stacking two the same day is only for fast groups).

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Off-camber ledges and growing rock ledges toward the rim—articulation and careful spotting; rear locker per RR4W.
  • Stock JL: Often doable with technique; Picture Rock positioning can feel exposed for passengers.
  • Gladiator 35s: Tire and locker stack helps; still a rough ride—plan suspension patience.

Flat Iron Mesa RR4W 6

Kane Creek corridor country south of Moab: mesa roads and canyon overlooks on a mix of old two-tracks. RR4W lists ~55 mi total / ~17 mi off highway, 35″+ tires, and front + rear lockers. Multiple named obstacles set the rating—some have bypasses, some do not. The optional Easter Egg Rock squeeze leads into a very narrow cliff-edge section that is not for anyone uncomfortable with exposure.

Typical time: about 6–9 hours for the full mesa tour.

Often combined with: usually its own Kane Creek day (Golden Spike / Chicken Corners country is the classic neighbor—confirm open status and group appetite). Not the same outing as Poison Spider (Potash side).

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Below RR4W’s posted minimums for this run—treat as leader discretion only, not a default group line.
  • Stock JL: Same story on tires/lockers versus RR4W call—possible in dry conditions with an experienced spotter, but not “by the book.”
  • Gladiator 35s + both lockers: Closest match to the club grid; still respect no-bypass spots and cliff exposure.

Sevenmile Rim RR4W 4

Leaves US-191 north of the UT-313 junction (~11 mi north of Moab), crosses old Cotter uranium workings, then runs rim roads with Entrada cliffs, Uranium Arch, Merrimac/Monitor slickrock, and may dip through Tusher Pass into Tusher Wash (overlaps Wipe-Out Hill country if the full fork is used). RR4W lists ~50 mi total / ~21 mi off highway—a big scenic day with moderate technicality.

Typical time: about 5–8 hours for the full published loop variant.

Often combined with: when the route uses Tusher / Big Mesa, you are in the same neighborhood as Wipe-Out Hill—some groups plan one trail and a future return for the other rather than same-day doubling.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Well matched; watch sidehill slickrock near Merrimac and sandy pulls late in the day.
  • Stock JL: Good fit for RR4W “4” with patience on sandy hills.
  • Gladiator 35s: Comfortable; still spot tail swing on tight rim turns.

3-D RR4W 4

Northwest of Moab around Hidden Canyon and Brink Spring: canyon bottom, rim, and a higher overlook (“three dimensions” of the same country). RR4W lists ~59 mi total / ~28 mi off highway—mostly pleasant family-style 4×4 with a few hills that can challenge stock tires. Highlights include Mean Hill (hogback with drop-offs both sides) and seasonal sand in the canyon bottom.

Typical time: about 6–9 hours (mileage-heavy day).

Often combined with: usually standalone northwest of town. Some trips tag a highway scenic on Utah 191 before/after—not wheeling on this page.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Generally aligned with RR4W minimums; air down for sand.
  • Stock JL: Often fine; may need multiple attempts on Airport View Hill at highway pressures.
  • Gladiator 35s: Easy cruise; still respect hogback exposure for passengers.

Dome Plateau RR4W 4

High mesa country north of the Colorado River and east of Arches, usually staged from the Dewey Bridge / Utah 128 side—RR4W lists ~98 mi total / ~30 mi off highway with long paved connectors. Forested plateau tops, mine-country scenery, arches along Entrada cliffs, dinosaur tracks, and a strong Colorado River overlook. Mostly dirt with one notable loose rock / ledge hill (“Again-and-Again Hill”) that may offer a guided bypass.

Typical time: about 8–11 hours (almost always a full day because of road miles).

Often combined with: Top of the World is the usual “neighbor” in trip planning (same river-corridor staging)—typically not same day unless you like pain.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Good match; fuel and time are the real constraints on this mileage.
  • Stock JL: Traction usually fine on a “4”; plan a full day and carry extra fuel if you tend to run short.
  • Gladiator 35s: Comfortable on obstacles; watch length on tight hill switchbacks.

Behind the Rocks, Tip-Toe RR4W 5

Scenic tour of Behind the Rocks that stays clear of the worst High Dive / White Knuckle obstacles on the hard route, but still intersects that system. RR4W lists ~53 mi total / ~33 mi off highway and calls for a rear locker; rating was bumped as some rocky descents eroded. Surfaces are mostly sandy dirt with occasional ledgy drainages—great arches-in-the-distance scenery.

Typical time: about 7–9 hours (long off-pavement mileage).

Often combined with: usually standalone south/behind-the-rim day. The full Behind the Rocks hard line is a different commitment—Tip-Toe is the “big scenic” variant on this page.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Ledgy washes reward patient lines and rear traction; skip if wet clay is smearing rocks.
  • Stock JL: Often workable on a “5” Tip-Toe with spotting; protect sheetmetal on any rock gardens.
  • Gladiator 35s: Traction is rarely the limiter; mind width on brushy two-tracks.

Hell’s Revenge - Tip-toe through Hell RR4W 4

RR4W lists this run as Hell’s Revenge, Tip-Toe Trip (trail id 37): their guided weekday flavor of Hell’s Revenge—most of the same slickrock with bypasses emphasized at the hair-raising obstacles, aimed at cautious drivers and near-stock rigs. Optional: if the whole group wants a harder line, many named obstacles still have primary routes (treat as RR4W “6” moves for equipment and spotting—see full Hell’s Revenge). Listed as ~16 mi total / ~12 mi off highway, 33″+, no lockers required on paper for the Tip-Toe baseline. This is still Hell’s Revenge geology (exposure, steep climbs); not a paved overlook.

Typical time: about 3–5 hours (similar footprint to full Hell’s Revenge, less obstacle queue time).

Often combined with: Fins and Things the same Sand Flats day is possible for a skills-building day (still long). Baby Lion’s Back fits May 6 evening after arrival or May 7 morning as a slickrock warm-up before Tip-toe—not as a late stack after a full Hell’s day.

Hardest parts vs your rigs:

  • 4Runner 33s: Reasonable entry to the Hell’s landscape if the group wants less obstacle drama than the full “6.”
  • Stock JL: Often the best fit for a Tip-Toe pace—still bring a spotter mindset.
  • Gladiator 35s: Easy mechanically; watch breakover on any optional harder spurs if the leader opens them.

Shafer Trail vs Dead Horse Point State Park

These names come up in the same Island in the Sky / Colorado River overlook conversations, but they are not the same place and not the same agency.

  • Shafer Trail is a steep, switchbacked 4WD road in Canyonlands National Park — Island in the Sky (NPS), often described as requiring high clearance and comfort with exposure. It needs a park pass / fees, follows NPS road rules (including seasonal closures or maintenance gates—check current alerts), and is a different planning problem than RR4W’s Sand Flats “club trail” grid. It is not inside Dead Horse Point State Park.
  • Dead Horse Point State Park is a Utah State Parks unit on the peninsula rim northwest of Moab—famous gooseneck overlooks, hiking and biking trails, and developed camping. Great for a scenic half-day or sunset; off-roading is not a Shafer-style descent through the park’s main visitation experience.

If you camp at Dead Horse, see Moab camping for Kayenta/Wingate notes. For Shafer, start from the official Canyonlands pages the day you go—conditions and rules change.

Shafer Trail (one-way emphasis): about 2–4 hours for cautious downhill/photo pacing (not counting Island in the Sky overlooks or the drive back around). Often planned with other Canyonlands paved viewpoints the same day.

Dead Horse Point State Park: about 1–3 hours for overlooks + short walks; pairs naturally with a Moab → park → back scenic loop. Combine with Shafer only as a big-picture itinerary (different agencies, different roads—see bullets above).

Extra: Baby Lion’s Back (Sand Flats) not BOH

Very short one-way Sand Flats practice fin near Hell’s Revenge / Fins—often cited ~15 minutes. Steep descent can end in a water pool after storms or snowmelt (hydrolock / deep wading risk). Third-party guides often call it moderate (~5); not the same publication as RR4W’s grid. For this trip: Wed May 6 evening after Moab arrival if time allows, or Thu May 7 morning as a slickrock warm-up before Tip-toe through Hell (see Glenn’s shortlist).

Typical time: about 20–45 minutes including staging, spotting, and turnaround (longer if you wait for other groups).

Often combined with: Hell’s Revenge or Fins and Things as a same-evening dessert (same Sand Flats pass mindset).

Hardest parts vs your rigs: Descent angle + cross-slope (pick the easier rider line); pool depth at the bottom for 4Runner/JL; Gladiator—watch tail swing at the turnaround.

Also BOH at RR4W ≤ 6, but not a Moab “town day”

Elephant Hill (Canyonlands Needles) is RR4W 5 on the same 2025 grid and is a Badge of Honor trail, but the trailhead is a long highway day from Moab and has NPS permits, timed access, and vehicle length rules—plan as its own national-park mission, not as filler between Sand Flats loops.

Typical time: about 6–10 hours from Moab-area lodging as a full Needles mission (drive + trail + permits).

Often combined with: other Canyonlands Needles hikes or overlooks the same trip—not with Hell’s Revenge same day.
