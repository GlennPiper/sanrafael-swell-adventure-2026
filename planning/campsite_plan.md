## May 2 (Saturday) -- Staging: Black Dragon Canyon

All three May-2 options are BLM dispersed camping -- no hotels or state parks. Group uses dispersed camping throughout the Swell portion.

- **Primary:** GPX `Camp site` at **38.93141, -110.42163** (0.80 km from Day-1 track start). Camp under cottonwoods up the Black Dragon Canyon access road. Free, BLM.
    - Access: I-70 Exit 145 (Black Dragon); gated side road on the N side; sandy, high-clearance recommended.
    - **NO camping in the canyon interior** (petroglyph panel area).
    - Facilities: none. No toilets, no water. Pack it all.
- **Secondary:** `Black Dragon Trailhead Flats` at **38.92676, -110.41852** (221 m from Day-1 trail start). BLM dispersed on the open flats right off I-70 at the Black Dragon exit. Free, BLM. Lower ambiance than the primary (highway noise), but dead simple to find for late arrivals and large enough for the full 11-rig group. Use as the overflow for anyone who arrives after dark, or consolidate all staging here if primary is crowded.
- **Tertiary:** `San Rafael Reef View Area` at **38.92111, -110.43187** (signed BLM pullout on I-70, ~1 mi SW of the Black Dragon trailhead; ~1.35 km / 0.85 mi from the Day-1 track start). Free, BLM. Source: <https://maps.app.goo.gl/UfmZrtjhDtXHCEjD6>.
    - Use when both primary and secondary are full, or when late arrivals want an obvious landmark pullout after dark.
    - Negligible backtrack in the morning -- hop back on I-70 eastbound to Exit 145 (Black Dragon) and start Day 1 as planned.
    - Freeway-adjacent (expect interstate noise). No designated pads -- scout on arrival for the flattest line-up for the group.

**Scout plan:** if a lead vehicle is ~30 min ahead of the group, they should pick a cluster of adjacent flat spots at the primary and mark with chem lights / reflective markers. If the primary is crowded, fall back to the secondary (at the trailhead) as a single consolidated staging spot rather than splitting across the two.

## May 3 (Sunday, end Day 1) -- Wedge Overlook cluster

Day-1 track ends at **39.09642, -110.75111** (Wedge Overlook viewpoint). The Wedge is **designated-sites-only** -- we must camp in a numbered site, no off-site dispersed allowed. Eight numbered sites (GPX-verified) are spread along the rim road 2-5 km N/NW of the overlook; all are first-come, $15/site honor system, no facilities. All 8 sites are now pinned on the Day-1 map.

- **Primary:** `Wedge Overlook Campsite #5` at **39.12445, -110.75133** (3.12 km from track end). Cluster anchor.
    - Sites #3, #4, #5, #6, #7 form a **tight cluster within 150 m** of each other -- perfect for splitting the group across 3-4 adjacent sites. All five cluster members show on the map as primary-tier pins.
    - GPX coords of the cluster:
        - #3: 39.12423, -110.74498
        - #4: 39.12748, -110.74781
        - #5: 39.12445, -110.75133 (pin)
        - #6: 39.12254, -110.75204
        - #7: 39.12563, -110.75521
    - Very windy up there -- tie everything down.
- **Secondary (equal-rank designated backups):** the remaining three numbered Wedge sites. Each stands on its own -- use any combination if the #3-#7 cluster is full.
    - **Secondary A -- `Wedge Overlook Camp #1`** at **39.10857, -110.70223** (east rim, ~4.4 km E of overlook). Farthest from the viewpoint but good sunrise exposure.
    - **Secondary B -- `Wedge Overlook Camp #2`** at **39.11221, -110.73341** (east rim, ~2 km WSW of #1). Pairs well with #1 if two rigs split east.
    - **Secondary C -- `Wedge Overlook Campsite #8`** at **39.11674, -110.75523** (southern outlier on the same rim road as #3-#7, ~900 m SSE of the cluster). Use it to extend the cluster if we need one extra, or as a standalone.
- **Tertiary (only if every Wedge site is occupied):** `Buckhorn Draw Campsite 1` at **39.16753, -110.73766** (7.99 km from track end). Developed FCFS, $15 ind / $50 group; vault toilets, fire rings, tables. GPX has sites 1, 7, 14, 15, 16, 17, 20, 23, 26, 30, 32 mapped. Requires backtracking down Buckhorn Draw Rd.

**Scout plan:** during the Buckhorn Petroglyphs mid-afternoon stop, send a lead vehicle ahead to the Wedge rim to claim 3-4 adjacent sites in the #3-#7 cluster. If all 5 cluster sites are taken, pick up #8 as a bolt-on before spreading out to #1/#2.

## May 4 (Monday, end Day 2) -- Tomsich Butte

Day-2 track ends at **38.69753, -110.97621** -- that's the **Tomsich Butte** area, not Family Butte. (Earlier drafts of this plan named Family Butte for the night; the route actually runs further SW to Tomsich.)

- **Primary:** `Tomsich Butte Camp` at **38.68282, -110.98900** (1.98 km from track end). Free BLM dispersed. Open flat area near the uranium mine ruins and Hondu Arch trailhead. Monday night should be uncrowded.
- **Secondary:** GPX `Camp` at **38.69237, -110.99904** (2.06 km from track end). Adjacent dispersed spot ~1 km NW of primary. Small footprint, 2-3 rigs.
- **Tertiary (BAIL EARLY):** `Family Butte Dispersed Camping` at **38.76868, -110.83217** (14.79 km off track end, ~mid-Day-2 at Family Butte Rd spur). Open dispersed area, room for 6-8 vehicles. **Use this only if Day 2 runs long** and you can't make it all the way to Tomsich before sunset. Day 3 would then start with a ~20 min drive Family Butte -> Reds Canyon -> Tomsich before the usual Day 3 POIs.

## Methodology note -- why these picks are trustworthy

Every Swell overnight coordinate in this plan is verified to be within **4 km of the relevant day's track start or end**, using a real GPX `campsite-24` waypoint. Tertiary fallbacks are explicitly flagged when they require backtracking or bailing early (Day 1 tertiary Buckhorn at 8 km; Day 2 tertiary Family Butte at 15 km as a "bail early" option).

This was not true in the earlier draft of this plan, which used descriptive names ("Family Butte Dispersed", "Wedge cluster") with fabricated lat/lon -- leading to Day-2 primary being **17 km off** its track end. The map visualization in `trip-itinerary.html` exposed this; verification scripts now confirm per-day proximity.

If you add a new camp or change a day's route, **snap coordinates to real GPX waypoints first** -- don't estimate. The GPX has 67 `campsite-24` points covering the entire Swell route.
