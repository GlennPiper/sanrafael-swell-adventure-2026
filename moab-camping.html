Moab-area camping reference

Working list for May 2026. Fees, reservation rules, and closures change often—confirm with land managers before you go.

Grandstaff Campground (SR 128): The BLM announced a partial closure of Grandstaff Campground (and related riverfront staging) from March 9 through October 31, 2026 for Colorado River Trail Gap construction. Verify current status before counting on those sites. BLM: Grandstaff Campground
Primitive camping near town: On BLM-managed lands, camping outside developed campgrounds is generally restricted within about 20 miles of Moab city limits except in specifically designated dispersed-site areas (signed posts). Always use marked sites, pack out human waste where required, and check the official camping map. BLM Moab camping map (PDF) · Discover Moab: BLM campgrounds summary

Sand Flats Recreation Area (BLM + Grand County)

Sand Flats is its own fee program: day-use pass plus per-night camping. National park passes usually do not cover Sand Flats (Access Pass is an exception per Recreation.gov). Phone (435) 259-2444.

Camping areas inside Sand Flats

Developed camping is in designated loops only (not random slickrock). Recreation.gov and Grand County describe nine individual campgrounds labeled A through H plus Juniper, with about 140 first-come, first-served vehicle sites total, plus an overflow area when open. Expect busy spring weekends; many campers report turnover mid-morning.

  • Loops A–D: Mix of RV-friendly and smaller sites; juniper and slickrock settings; pit toilets.
  • Loop E (“Echo”): Smaller loop; includes two reservable group sites (E-1, E-10) alongside individual FCFS sites.
  • Loops F & G (tent / high-clearance): More exposed fins; loop F often cited as 4×4 recommended for some spurs—scout first.
  • Loop H: Additional FCFS sites (often referenced as tent-biased in visitor write-ups).
  • Juniper loop: Separate loop name inside Sand Flats (distinct from the BLM “Juniper” campground on the Colorado Riverway list).
  • Overflow: Opens seasonally when demand is high; same fee structure when staffed.

Reservable group sites (six total)

All six are booked on Recreation.gov (typically shown as $80/night in gateway materials). Sites are clustered in Datura (A, B, C, D) and Echo (E-1, E-10). Parking limits differ by site (smaller lots on C and E-10).

Designated dispersed camping (BLM, signed sites)

These are not developed campgrounds. Camp only at signed posts (brown markers with tent symbols). Unless a toilet is provided, you need a portable toilet or approved waste-containment and must pack out waste. Popular pods fill fast.

Utahraptor State Park (incl. Willow Springs / Fossil Flats corridor)

Formerly informal BLM camping along Willow Springs Road is now inside Utahraptor State Park. The park operates two camping products: Gastonia Campground (partial hookups, showers, reservable) and Fossil Flats improved primitive camping (Willow Springs and Dalton Wells access per park signage—camp only in designated rings). Fees change by season and site type; use the official fee page before budgeting.

Developed BLM campgrounds (Moab Field Office)

All have tables & fire rings and vault toilets unless noted; potable water is rare—plan to haul from Moab. Most are FCFS; some have reservable group loops. Official site counts come from the BLM Moab camping PDF.
