Fuel Plan

Bottom line: The San Rafael Swell route is ~225 mi of off-pavement dirt/trail with zero fuel inside the Swell. Budget ~18-26 gal of fuel inside the Swell alone for a typical full-size 4x4. Anyone whose tank range is <250 mi should plan to carry jerry cans or make a ~50-mi fueling detour to Castle Dale, Emery, or Hanksville mid-route.

Caravan Considerations

  • Slowest vehicle sets pace -- MPG estimates above are per-vehicle; convoy speed is bound by the slowest rig, which can improve MPG for faster rigs (less spirited driving) but hurt it for the slowest (engine working harder).
  • Recovery = fuel -- stuck vehicles, winching, spotter revving = +0.5-1.5 gal of unexpected burn. Build in ~2 gal buffer for group recovery events.
  • Pre-trip fueling protocol: everyone fuels at the same station in Green River on May 2. No one starts the route with <90% tank.
  • Daily morning check-in: each driver reports remaining fuel; anyone <40% at start of day alerts group lead.

Flagged Risks

  1. No fuel in the Swell -- if a vehicle reaches ~1/4 tank at Day 2 or Day 3, the Castle Dale or Hanksville detour is MANDATORY; don't push through hoping for the best.
  2. Behind the Reef -- slow technical trail with high fuel burn; fuel tank should be >50% at Miner's Cabin (mi 184).
  3. Sand -- any sand pulled (Black Dragon Canyon wash, North Temple Wash) will hurt MPG more than rocky sections. In wet conditions sand also fills the undercarriage.
  4. Spare fuel container safety -- metal jerry cans on rooftop / exterior preferred over plastic; keep away from exhaust. NATO-style 5-gal cans are the standard.
  5. Fuel in 2026 -- prices fluctuate; budget ~$4.25-$5.50/gal for the trip at current Utah averages. Full-trip fuel cost ~$500-$650 per vehicle.
