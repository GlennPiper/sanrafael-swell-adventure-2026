River crossing — Fuller Bottom (Day 2 POI)

The Day 2 itinerary stop River crossing is the Fuller Bottom ford of the San Rafael River, inside the Upper San Rafael River Wildlife Management Area (Utah DWR, Fuller Bottom unit) in the Little Grand Canyon. BLM also labels this access as Fullers Bottom Trailhead.

We approach and leave on the BLM Fuller Bottom road (NF-3516 / BLM 6767), which becomes Little Wedge Road (BLM 629) after the crossing. The North Salt Wash Local Trail (BLM 6770) is touched only briefly on the south side — our route skirts it rather than following it.

River gauges & weather (use these first)

Official USGS monitoring pages plus static discharge graphs help you compare upstream vs downstream San Rafael flow. Pair that with a pinpoint NWS forecast for the Fuller Bottom ford and for the headwaters.

BLM flow thresholds at Fuller Bottom

  • < 50 cfs — Hike-friendly. BLM advises on-foot crossings only below this.
  • 50–120 cfs — Fordable for vehicles, but getting harder; pick a line.
  • 120–180 cfs — Difficult ford; deepest crossings on the canyon hike approach ~3 ft near 180.
  • > 180 cfs — Don’t wade. For context, BLM suggests small craft only above ~200 cfs, larger craft above ~500 cfs.

Place names and geography

  • Fuller Bottom — local and official name for this river bottom and ford; named for homesteader Tom Fuller. BLM signs it as Fullers Bottom Trailhead; Utah DWR manages it as the Fuller Bottom unit of the Upper San Rafael River WMA.
  • Upper San Rafael River Wildlife Management Area — Utah DWR Southeastern Region. Two units: Hambrick Bottom (upstream, at the confluence where Ferron / Cottonwood / Huntington Creeks form the San Rafael) and Fuller Bottom (our crossing). Public access on the Fuller Bottom unit rides on a 1982 perpetual easement granted by Utah Power & Light.
  • Little Grand Canyon — the canyon reach of the San Rafael we enter at Fuller Bottom; The Wedge Overlook looks down on it from the north rim.
  • Salt Wash / North Salt Wash — south-side tributary system; our route only touches the North Salt Wash Local Trail (BLM 6770) briefly after the ford, then turns onto Little Wedge Road.
  • Fuller Bottom Road / Little Wedge Road — the BLM road we use. It intersects the Buckhorn Wash road near the Wedge turn-off on the north side; south of the river it forks and exits the WMA (we take the Little Wedge / BLM 629 branch).

Flow direction and “upstream” weather

  • At Fuller Bottom, the San Rafael River flows generally down-canyon toward the east / southeast, into the Little Grand Canyon and eventually to the Green River.
  • For main-stem runoff, watch the Wasatch Plateau headwaters: Ferron Creek, Cottonwood Creek, and Huntington Creek converge near Hambrick Bottom to form the San Rafael, which then passes through Fuller Bottom.
  • A storm over those creeks is what drives the rise you see first on the Castle Dale gauge, and later, attenuated, on the Green River gauge.
  • A local thunderstorm near the ford can spike mud, bank firmness, and Salt Wash side-flows without a proportional move on the main-stem gauges.

How to read conditions before you arrive

  • USGS streamflow on the San Rafael — see River gauges & weather at the top of this page (graphs + monitoring links). No gauge on Salt Wash at the Fuller Bottom ford.
  • BLM fording bands (recap): hikers <50 cfs; ~120 cfs difficult; ~180 cfs ≈ 3 ft at deepest.
  • Weather — use the pinpoint + Castle Dale NWS links at the top for the ford and the Wasatch Plateau headwaters (Ferron / Cottonwood / Huntington).
  • BLM Price Field Office — 435-636-3600 — road status, closures, seasonal guidance.
  • Utah DWR Southeastern Region (Price) — regional office contacts. WMA day-use is open; motorized use is limited to the BLM road corridor through the WMA.
  • Trip reports — qualitative only: BLM Fullers Bottom Trailhead, Trails Offroad — Fuller Bottom, NRP — Fullers Bottom to Swinging Bridge, Crockett run write-up, Sean Ryan 50k, ADVENTR Little Grand Canyon.

Limits

  • No public sensor for ford depth at Fuller Bottom — BLM bands are indicative, not a substitute for eyes on the water and the bank.
  • Salt Wash and local thunderstorms can change the approaches (mud, soft sand, side-wash flow) without a big move on Castle Dale.
  • Fuller Bottom Road is a class D BLM road with no designated maintenance; condition varies with recent weather and last passage.
  • Stay on the BLM road corridor through the WMA; confirm route legality and seasonal rules with BLM / DWR.
