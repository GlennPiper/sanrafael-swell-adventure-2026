Slot canyons & Day 3 hikes (San Rafael Swell)

Companion to Trip Reference — Day 3 hikes (works offline in the PWA) and poi_decisions.md (locked POI triage). Summarizes trailhead → payoff mileage, online references, group constraints (soft ~1 mi one-way to a turnaround), and trip-specific opinions for 2026.

Interpreting the distance guideline

The group uses a soft limit of about one mile one-way to a turnaround or destination, i.e. often ~2 mi round trip. This is a comfort target, not a hard rule.

Trip-specific takes (2026 group)

Little Wild Horse Canyon — Would be #1 if approaching from the Goblin Valley / trailhead side; from Behind the Reef Road the tightest narrows are several miles of hiking before the classic “slot” feel. Plan: skip for this trip unless the whole group wants the full ~8 mi LWH + Bell Canyon loop (great hike, but scrambling — not everyone will be comfortable).

Bell Canyon — Not as narrow as Little Wild Horse. An out-and-back in Bell could work but is not the first choice for this trip.

Crack Canyon — Strong personal favorite: great hike. Caution: near N 38°37.531′, W 110°44.290′ (~38.6255, -110.7382 WGS84) there is about a 10 ft drop to descend and later re-ascend on the out-and-back — assess the party before committing. Dispersed camping may be available just past Crack Canyon trailhead (verify current BLM rules and site conditions).

Wild Horse Canyon — AllTrails — Wild Horse Canyon. Other options (Chute, Crack, WHW) fit this trip better for most goals.

Chute Canyon — Not very narrow at first (some reports ~100–200 ft wide); does not start to tighten for roughly the first mile. Easier hiking — may be the best “soft” slot-adjacent option if abilities are unknown.

Wild Horse Window — Well known; big landscape / arch payoff, less “slot canyon” excitement. Recommended for the itinerary. Park boundary note: the hike is not inside Goblin Valley State Park. You use Temple Mountain / Goblin Valley roads as access from Hwy 24; the trailhead and arch are on BLM — no state park fee for this hike if you do not enter the park gate. Group logistics: plan to set up camp (Temple Mountain area) before this hike, then carpool to the trailhead so fewer rigs compete for the small parking area. (Confirm with current maps; sources: Utah.com — Wild Horse Window, HikingInUtah — Wild Horse Window.)

Bottom line for Day 3

  1. Default hike (narrative): Wild Horse Window (fits mileage guideline; no slot flood risk).
  2. Easier canyon walk (tactical): Chute Canyon (mild; wide early) — on the itinerary as Hike (tactical), checked by default; uncheck if the group skips it.
  3. Stronger slot (tactical): Crack Canyon (watch 10 ft obstacle and party fitness) — same badge/checkbox behavior as WHW and Chute.
  4. Little Wild Horse: Prefer skip on this route direction unless committing to full LWH/Bell loop and scrambling is OK for everyone.
